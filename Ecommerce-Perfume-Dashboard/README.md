E-Commerce Perfume Sales Dashboard


Project Objectives

Analyse perfume sales performance across different brands, product types, and locations.
Identify the top-performing perfume brands based on revenue and units sold.
Monitor average product pricing and total listings in the e-commerce marketplace.
Understand customer preferences by analysing perfume categories such as Eau de Toilette, Eau de Parfum, and Cologne.
Compare revenue contributions across brands and regions to support better business decisions.
Create an interactive dashboard that allows users to filter data dynamically by brand, price range, and product type.


Data Cleaning & Preparation

The dataset was cleaned and transformed in Power Query before visualisation. The following steps were performed:

Removed duplicate records to ensure accurate reporting.
Checked for missing or null values and handled them appropriately.
Standardised brand and product names for consistency.
Corrected data types for columns such as revenue, units sold, and prices.
Filtered out irrelevant or incomplete records.
Created calculated measures using DAX for:
Total Revenue
Total Units Sold
Average Price
Total Listings
Formatted numerical values into readable metrics (K and M formats).


Key Insights

Versace and Calvin Klein generated the highest revenue among all brands.
Eau de Toilette accounted for the largest share of perfume sales.
Certain locations contributed significantly more revenue than others, showing regional demand concentration.
Top-selling perfumes consistently outperformed other products in units sold.
The dashboard revealed strong relationships between brand popularity and overall revenue generation.


Recommendations

Increase inventory and marketing efforts for high-performing brands such as Versace and Calvin Klein.
Focus promotional campaigns on Eau de Toilette products since they dominate sales volume.
Expand operations or targeted advertising in high-revenue locations to maximise profitability.
Review low-performing brands and products to determine whether pricing, marketing, or product positioning needs improvement.
Use customer purchasing trends to optimise future product listings and pricing strategies.
Continuously monitor sales trends using the dashboard for faster business decision-making.
